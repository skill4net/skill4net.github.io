Options trading - Long Call

Posted on 2020-08-22 In Options trading , FinTech , Personal Investment]
Options trading - Long Call, how to profit or lose, charting

The Long Call option

Buying a long call option contract will give the investor the right to purchase the underlying asset (stock or ETF) at the strike price. [1][2][3][4][5][6]

If the invester believes the asset price will increases quickly and significantly in a short period of time, the long call option will let the investor possible profit from higher asset price, gain leverage with cheaper option cost.

The Long Call option trading is a simple strategy for beginner to try option trading. It is a Limited Risk strategy with maximum loss as Premium Paid + Commissions Paid, and no Assignment Risk.

For example, on 07-08-2020, to buy BAC Call 16-10-2020 with strike price $31, the premium was $0.32. The broker commission is about $0.65 to $1 per contract. So we will pay about $33 per contract (for 100 stock shares). If we do not close this contract before the expiration date, e.g. 16-10-2020, the maximum loss is $33.

To learn the option trading, we have to choose a strategy to start with. When we plan to buy a long call option, we will learn a lot of option trading concepts and terminologies, such as Strike Price, Premium, Expiration, Assignment, To Exercise, Implied Volatility, Delta, and many many.[7][8][9]

Plan $30 to $60 for each option deal. Many options with 45 to 60 days to expiration are near $25 to $30 per contract (or premium is near $0.25 to $0.30). Select the stocks or ETFs that are in up trend, in general, the stock price should higher than its MA5[10]. Find the suitable strike price and premium to match our buying budget.

Note: Due to the securities markets operating acts in New Zealand, many overseas brokers decline the New Zealand residents (physical resident address in New Zealand) to trade options. Before opening new account in any overseas broker, do contact the broker to clerify their policy and control.

How to profit or lose with the Long Call option

Let’s look at the example for the stock Bank of America Corporation (BAC).

In the end of July 2020, we found the BAC price kept moving up. On 06-08-2020 or 07-08-2020, we decided to buy BAC stocks or Long Call options. The following timeline shows how the stock price and Call premium (Expiry 16-10-202 Strike price $31) floating over 10 trading days.

(1) When stock price falled on 12-08-2020, if we sold the stock or close the Long Call options, would profit +2.4% on stocks, or +28.1% on options.

(2) When stock price kept falling on 14-08-2020 and lower than the Simple Moving Average (SMA, or MA) 5 days price (MA5), if we sold the stock or close the Long Call options, would profit +1.4% on stocks, or +9.4% on options.

(3) If we love the stock or the option and keep holding it, on 20-08-2020 would lose -3.9% on stocks, or -62.5% on options.

Do and do not, points to consider

(1) Do not love any stock or option, trading is all about profit. Take profit and quit.

As the example above, if we closed the options on 11-08-2020, would gain near +37.5% profit. Although we don’t know that was the peak, if we were satisfied with +25% to +30% gain, we would own the profit.

(2) Do take stop-loss action when stock price falled under MA5.

(3) Options trading has very high risk. After placing trading order, do check the stock price trend every day. Do configure stop-loss settings in trading software (or mobile app) to keep our money safe.

(4) Consider to close the option in 20 days before the expiration date. Don’t let the option expired to avoid any unexpected risk.

(5) Do not expect buying on dips, selling on peaks. Consider buying on averages, selling on averages.
